During your travel dates, Tbilisi will experience cold winter weather.

### 6. Tourist Attractions

Here are the top tourist attractions and areas to visit in and around Tbilisi:

*   **Old Town of Tbilisi:** A historic district with narrow streets, colorful houses, traditional Georgian architecture, sulfur baths, and vibrant cafes. Ideal for walking, photography, and experiencing local culture and food.
*   **Bridge of Peace:** A modern, bow-shaped pedestrian bridge over the Mtkvari River, known for its unique glass and steel design and LED lighting. Great for evening photography.
*   **Holy Trinity Cathedral of Tbilisi (Sameba):** The main cathedral of the Georgian Orthodox Church, an impressive architectural marvel offering panoramic views of the city.
*   **Narikala Fortress:** An ancient fortress overlooking Tbilisi and the Mtkvari River, accessible by cable car. Offers stunning views, especially at sunset.
*   **Mother of Georgia Statue:** A monumental statue symbolizing the Georgian national character, located on Sololaki Hill, near Narikala Fortress.
*   **Rustaveli Avenue:** The main avenue of Tbilisi, home to important government buildings, cultural institutions, shops, and cafes.
*   **Day trip to Kazbegi (Stepantsminda):** A picturesque mountain region in the Greater Caucasus, famous for the Gergeti Trinity Church with stunning views of Mount Kazbek. Perfect for hiking, photography, and experiencing heavy snowfall in December.
*   **Day trip to Mtskheta:** One of Georgia's oldest cities and a UNESCO World Heritage site, featuring ancient monasteries and cathedrals like Svetitskhoveli Cathedral and Jvari Monastery. Rich in history and spiritual significance.
*   **Day trip to Gudauri:** A popular ski resort in the Greater Caucasus Mountains, offering opportunities for skiing, snowboarding, and other snow activities. Ideal for those interested in active snow sports.

### 7. Day-wise Itinerary

**Day 1: Arrival in Tbilisi & Old Town Exploration**
*   **Morning:** Arrive at Tbilisi International Airport (TBS). Transfer to your hotel, check-in, and relax.
*   **Afternoon:** Begin exploring the charming **Old Town of Tbilisi**. Wander through its narrow, cobblestone streets, admire the colorful balconies, and soak in the historic atmosphere.
*   **Evening:** Enjoy a traditional Georgian dinner at a local restaurant in the Old Town, savoring dishes like Khinkali (dumplings) and Khachapuri (cheese bread).
*   **Attractions:** Old Town of Tbilisi.
*   **Recommended Hotel:** Hotel near Old Town (e.g., Hotel Frida).
*   **Restaurant Suggestions:** Shavi Lomi, Puri Guliani.
*   **Local Transport:** Taxi from the airport, then walking in Old Town.
*   **Weather Precautions:** Dress warmly for outdoor walking.
*   **Travel Tips:** Exchange currency at the airport or a reliable exchange office. Purchase a local SIM card for connectivity.

**Day 2: Cultural Immersion & City Views**
*   **Morning:** Visit the majestic **Holy Trinity Cathedral of Tbilisi (Sameba)**, marvel at its grandeur, and enjoy panoramic views of the city.
*   **Afternoon:** Take a cable car up to **Narikala Fortress** for breathtaking views of Tbilisi. Explore the fortress ruins and visit the **Mother of Georgia Statue**.
*   **Evening:** Walk across the illuminated **Bridge of Peace** and enjoy the modern architecture. Have dinner at a restaurant with views of the Mtkvari River.
*   **Attractions:** Holy Trinity Cathedral, Narikala Fortress, Mother of Georgia Statue, Bridge of Peace.
*   **Recommended Hotel:** Hotel near Old Town.
*   **Restaurant Suggestions:** Funicular Restaurant Complex (for views), Barbarestan (for traditional Georgian cuisine).
*   **Local Transport:** Metro or taxi to Sameba, cable car to Narikala, walking.
*   **Weather Precautions:** Be prepared for cold and windy conditions at higher elevations.
*   **Travel Tips:** Wear comfortable shoes for walking and exploring. Carry a power bank for your phone.

**Day 3: Day Trip to Mtskheta & Jvari Monastery**
*   **Morning:** Embark on a day trip to **Mtskheta**, the ancient capital of Georgia and a UNESCO World Heritage site. Visit **Svetitskhoveli Cathedral**.
*   **Afternoon:** Continue to **Jvari Monastery**, perched on a hilltop, offering stunning views of the confluence of the Mtkvari and Aragvi rivers.
*   **Evening:** Return to Tbilisi. Enjoy a relaxed evening, perhaps visiting a sulfur bath in Abanotubani for a unique experience.
*   **Attractions:** Mtskheta (Svetitskhoveli Cathedral), Jvari Monastery.
*   **Recommended Hotel:** Hotel near Old Town.
*   **Restaurant Suggestions:** Salobie Bia (Mtskheta for local food), or back in Tbilisi.
*   **Local Transport:** Hire a private car/taxi or join a guided tour for the day trip.
*   **Weather Precautions:** Roads to monasteries can be icy; ensure your transport is suitable.
*   **Travel Tips:** Learn a few basic Georgian phrases like "Gamarjoba" (hello) and "Madloba" (thank you).

**Day 4: Winter Wonderland in Kazbegi**
*   **Morning:** Take a scenic drive along the Georgian Military Highway to **Kazbegi (Stepantsminda)**. The journey itself is breathtaking with snow-covered landscapes.
*   **Afternoon:** Hike or take a 4x4 vehicle to the iconic **Gergeti Trinity Church**, offering unparalleled views of Mount Kazbek, especially beautiful with snow. Enjoy photography amidst the snowy mountains.
*   **Evening:** Return to Tbilisi.
*   **Attractions:** Georgian Military Highway, Gergeti Trinity Church, Mount Kazbek.
*   **Recommended Hotel:** Hotel near Old Town.
*   **Restaurant Suggestions:** Local guesthouses in Stepantsminda for authentic mountain food, or back in Tbilisi.
*   **Local Transport:** Hire a private car with an experienced driver or join a small group tour.
*   **Weather Precautions:** This trip is highly weather-dependent. Roads can be closed due to heavy snowfall. Dress in extreme layers, including waterproof and insulated gear.
*   **Travel Tips:** Start early to maximize daylight. Check road conditions before departing.

**Day 5: Skiing and Snow Fun in Gudauri**
*   **Morning:** Head to **Gudauri**, Georgia's premier ski resort.
*   **Afternoon:** Enjoy skiing, snowboarding, or simply playing in the snow. Take a cable car ride for panoramic views of the snowy mountains.
*   **Evening:** Return to Tbilisi.
*   **Attractions:** Gudauri Ski Resort.
*   **Recommended Hotel:** Hotel near Old Town.
*   **Restaurant Suggestions:** On-slope restaurants in Gudauri, or back in Tbilisi.
*   **Local Transport:** Marshrutka (minibus) or private transfer to Gudauri.
*   **Weather Precautions:** Wear appropriate ski gear. Be aware of avalanche warnings and stick to marked trails.
*   **Travel Tips:** Rent ski equipment in Gudauri. Consider a beginner's lesson if new to skiing.

*   **Afternoon:** Discover the Flea Market at Dry Bridge for unique souvenirs, antiques, and local crafts.
*   **Evening:** Enjoy a farewell dinner at a highly-rated restaurant, perhaps trying more local delicacies or international cuisine.
*   **Attractions:** Rustaveli Avenue, Georgian National Museum, Dry Bridge Market.
*   **Recommended Hotel:** Hotel near Old Town.
*   **Restaurant Suggestions:** Cafe Gabriadze, Keto and Kote.

### 9. Travel Tips

*   **Visa:** Check visa requirements for Indian citizens traveling to Georgia well in advance.
*   **Currency:** The local currency is the Georgian Lari (GEL). It's advisable to carry some cash for smaller purchases, though cards are widely accepted in cities.
*   **Language:** Georgian is the official language. English is spoken in tourist areas, but learning a few basic Georgian phrases will be appreciated.
*   **Connectivity:** Purchase a local SIM card upon arrival for easy navigation and communication.
*   **Dress Code:** When visiting churches and monasteries, women should cover their heads and wear skirts (or trousers that cover the knees). Men should wear trousers.
*   **Safety:** Tbilisi is generally a safe city. However, always be aware of your surroundings and take standard precautions against petty theft.
*   **Food & Drink:** Tap water is generally safe to drink in Tbilisi. Be adventurous and try local dishes and Georgian wine, which is famous worldwide.
*   **Tipping:** Tipping is not mandatory but appreciated for good service in restaurants and cafes (around 10%).
*   **Winter Travel:** Be flexible with your itinerary, especially for mountain excursions, as heavy snowfall can sometimes lead to road closures. Always check weather and road conditions before heading out.
*   **Photography:** Georgia offers incredible photographic opportunities, from ancient architecture to stunning mountain landscapes. Carry extra batteries and memory cards.
